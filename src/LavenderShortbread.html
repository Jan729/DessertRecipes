Lavender Shortbread

Prep time: 20 mins
Total time: 45 mins
Yield: About two dozen cookies
These fragrant cookies are perfect for afternoon tea and are very easy to make.

With this recipe, I bake the shortbread in one large, thin rectangle and cut the shortbread into triangles after it's done baking. This means you'll always get cookies with crisp edges without needed to chill the dough or worry about the cookies spreading out in the oven.

Ingredients:

  • 1 1/2 cups unsalted butter, room temperature
  • 3/4 cup white sugar
  • 1 tbsp dried lavender
  • 1 tsp lemon zest
  • 2 3/4 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 tsp table salt

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 325°F.
  2. Cream the butter and sugar in a medium bowl until fluffy.
  3. Stir in the lavender, lemon zest and salt.
  4. Sift the flour into the bowl and stir to combine.
  5. Plop the dough onto a large baking tray lined with parchment paper. Use a rolling pin or your hands to smooth out the dough into a large rectangle 1/4" thick.
  6. Bake for 20-25 minutes or until the edges of the shortbread begin to lightly brown. (My oven takes 24 minutes.)
  7. While the tray is just out of the oven, cut the shortbread into triangles (or other simple shapes).
