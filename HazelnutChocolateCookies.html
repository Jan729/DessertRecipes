Hazelnut Chocolate Chunk Cookies

Prep time: 30 mins
Total time: 60 mins
Yield: 24-32 cookies (depending on how big you make them)
I came up with this recipe when I found some chocolate hazelnut bars lying around the house and decided to turn them into cookies. They turned out better than I expected! The chopped chocolate bar worked well because the hazelnut pieces were coated with melted chocolate inside of the cookie, and the chocolate pieces were gooier than if I had used chocolate chips. Since most of the recipes I found online used chocolate chips instead of bars, I used several of the recipes as inspiration and modified the ingredients to accommodate my chocolate bar's ratio of chocolate to nuts. I added a little more flour to balance out the higher moisture content in the chocolate bar compared to the chocolate chips.

Substitution note:
If you like a crispier cookie, you can substitute the nutty chocolate bars with 3/4 cups chocolate chips, 3/4 cups chopped nuts of your choice and use 1 cup of flour instead of 1 1/8 cups. I think the chocolate chips melt to a lesser degree than the chopped chocolate pieces and keeps the cookie's orginal shape better.

Ingredients:

  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter
  • 1/2 cup brown sugar
  • 1/2 cup white sugar
  • 1 egg, room temperature
  • 1/2 tsp vanilla extract
  • 1/4 cup rolled or old-fashioned oats
  • 1 1/8 cup all-purpose flour
  • 2 1/2 tsp baking powder
  • 1/4 tsp table salt
  • 2 bars (200g) of hazelnut chocolate bars, coarsely chopped

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 325°F.
  2. In a large bowl, mix the butter, brown sugar and white sugar until creamy.
  3. Mix in the egg and vanilla extract.
  4. In a separate medium-sized bowl, mix the oats, flour, baking powder, and salt.
  5. Pour the dry ingredients into the wet ingredients and stir to combine.
  6. Fold in the chocolate hazelnut chunks.
  7. Scoop out 1 heaped tablespoon of dough per cookie and space 1 inch apart on a baking tray or cookie sheet lined with parchment paper.
  8. Bake for 15-25 minutes, or until an inserted toothpick comes out without any dough pieces on it (melted chocolate is fine).
